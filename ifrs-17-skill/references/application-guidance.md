## Definition of an Insurance Contract

### Uncertain future event (B3–B5)
At least one of the following is uncertain at inception:
- (a) Probability of insured event occurring
- (b) When it will occur
- (c) How much entity will pay

Covers discovery of loss during contract term (B4) and events already occurred but with uncertain financial effect (B5).

### Payments in kind (B6)
Insurance contracts include those settled in kind (e.g., replacing stolen goods, providing medical services). Fixed-fee service contracts meeting paragraph 8 conditions may apply IFRS 15 or IFRS 17.

### Insurance risk vs other risks (B7–B16)
- **Insurance risk**: Risk other than financial risk, transferred from holder to issuer (B7)
- **Financial risk**: Changes in interest rates, prices, FX rates, indices, credit ratings — excludes non-financial variables specific to a party (B8)
- Contracts exposing issuer to financial risk without significant insurance risk are NOT insurance contracts (B7)
- **Lapse/persistency risk** and **expense risk** are NOT insurance risk (B14)
- Mutual entities: the mutual is a separate entity accepting risk from each policyholder (B16)

### Significant insurance risk (B17–B23)
- Significant if insured event could cause issuer to pay significant additional amounts in ANY single scenario with commercial substance (B18)
- Even if probability is extremely low or expected PV of contingent cash flows is small (B18)
- Must have possibility of a loss on PV basis (B19) — BUT reinsurance transferring substantially all insurance risk is deemed significant
- Assessed contract by contract (B22)
- Additional amounts exclude: loss of ability to charge for future service, waiver of cancellation charges on death, payments for events not causing significant loss to holder, possible reinsurance recoveries (B21)

### Examples of insurance contracts (B26):
Theft/damage, liability, life insurance, annuities, disability/medical, surety/fidelity/performance bonds, third-party product warranties, title insurance, travel insurance, catastrophe bonds, climate/geological insurance swaps

### Examples of NON-insurance contracts (B27):
Investment contracts without significant insurance risk, contracts returning all risk to policyholder, self-insurance, gambling contracts, pure derivatives, credit guarantees without loss requirement, weather derivatives (non-specific), catastrophe bonds (non-specific)

## Separating Components

### Investment components (B31–B32)
Distinct if BOTH:
- (a) Not highly interrelated with insurance component (measured independently; lapse of one doesn't cause lapse of other)
- (b) Equivalent contract sold or could be sold separately in same market/jurisdiction

### Distinct goods/services (B33–B35)
- Not distinct if: (a) cash flows/risks highly interrelated with insurance components, AND (b) entity provides significant integration service

### Insurance acquisition cash flows (B35A–B35D)
- Allocate directly attributable acquisition cash flows to current group and expected renewal groups using systematic and rational method (B35A)
- Revise allocation at end of each reporting period for changes in assumptions (B35B)
- Impairment: reduce carrying amount so it doesn't exceed expected net cash inflow for related group (B35D)

## Measurement

### Estimates of Future Cash Flows

#### Unbiased estimates (B37–B41)
- Objective: expected value (probability-weighted mean) of full range of outcomes
- Starting point: range of scenarios specifying amount, timing, probability
- Need not develop every explicit scenario if estimate is consistent with measurement objective
- Include catastrophic losses under existing contracts; exclude possible future contracts (B40)
- Information sources: reported claims, contract characteristics, historical data (adjusted for current conditions), reinsurance/market prices (B41)

#### Market variables and non-market variables (B42–B53)
- **Market variables**: Observable in or derived from markets (prices, interest rates)
- **Non-market variables**: All others (claim frequency/severity, mortality rates)
- Market variable estimates must be consistent with observable market prices; maximise observable inputs (B44)
- **Replicating portfolio**: If exists, may use fair value instead of explicit cash flow estimation (B46–B47)
- Non-market variable estimates: reflect all reasonable evidence, internal and external; give more weight to more persuasive information (B49–B50)
- Non-market variable probabilities must not contradict observable market variables (B51)

#### Current estimates (B54–B60)
- Review and update estimates each reporting period
- Changes in estimates must faithfully represent changes in conditions
- Post-reporting-period events that resolve uncertainty do not provide evidence of conditions at reporting date (B55 — IAS 10 principle)
- Current estimates ≠ most recent actual experience (investigate reasons for changes) (B56–B57)
- Include information about trends (B58)
- Inflation: correlated with interest rates; estimates must be consistent (B59)
- Do not anticipate future legislation changes until substantively enacted (B60)

#### Cash flows within the contract boundary (B61–B71)

**General principle (B61):** Estimates shall include all cash flows within the boundary of an existing contract and no other cash flows.

**Policyholder options (B62):** Many contracts have features enabling policyholders to take actions changing amounts (renewal, surrender, conversion options, options to stop paying premiums). Measurement shall reflect, on an expected value basis, the entity's current estimates of how policyholders will exercise options, and the risk adjustment shall reflect how actual behaviour may differ from expected. Shall not assume 100% probability of surrender or continuation — must use expected values even for single-contract groups.

**Renewals required by contract (B63):** When an issuer is required to renew or continue the contract, apply paragraph 34 to assess whether renewal premiums/cash flows are within the boundary of the original contract.

**Practical ability to reprice (B64):** An entity has the practical ability to set a price that fully reflects risks (paragraph 34) if it can price as it would for a new contract with the same characteristics, or amend benefits to match the price. Also has practical ability if it can reprice the portfolio even if not each individual policyholder. When assessing practical ability, consider all risks that would be considered when underwriting equivalent contracts on the renewal date. Reassess the boundary at each reporting period end for changes in circumstances affecting substantive rights and obligations.

**Included (B65):**
- Premiums and related cash flows
- Claims (reported, IBNR, future within boundary)
- Payments varying with underlying items
- Embedded derivative payments (if not separated)
- Allocated insurance acquisition cash flows
- Claim handling costs
- Contractual benefits in kind
- Policy administration/maintenance costs (including recurring commissions)
- Transaction-based taxes and levies
- Fiduciary tax payments/receipts
- Recovery potential (salvage, subrogation)
- Investment activity costs (to extent enhancing insurance benefits or providing investment service)
- Allocated fixed/variable overheads directly attributable to fulfilling contracts
- Other costs specifically chargeable to policyholder

**Excluded (B66):**
- Investment returns (recognised separately)
- Reinsurance cash flows (recognised separately)
- Cash flows outside contract boundary (future contracts)
- Non-attributable costs (product development, training)
- Abnormal waste costs
- Income tax (except fiduciary or policyholder-chargeable)
- Inter-component cash flows not affecting policyholder payments
- Cash flows from separated components

#### Pre-recognition assets and liabilities (B66A):
Before a group is recognised, an entity may be required to recognise an asset or liability for cash flows related to the group (other than insurance acquisition cash flows) — either because of the occurrence of cash flows or requirements of another IFRS Standard. Cash flows are "related to the group" if they would have been included in the fulfilment cash flows at initial recognition had they been paid or received after that date. To apply paragraph 38(c)(ii), the entity shall derecognise such an asset or liability to the extent it would not be recognised separately from the group if the cash flow or IFRS application had occurred at the date of initial recognition.

#### Contracts affecting other groups' cash flows (B67–B71)
Some contracts require the policyholder to share returns on the same specified pool of underlying items with policyholders of other contracts, where either: (i) the policyholder bears a reduction from payments to others (including guarantees), or (ii) other policyholders bear a reduction from payments to this policyholder (B67).

For such contracts, the fulfilment cash flows of each group reflect the extent to which the entity is affected by expected cash flows to policyholders in that group or another group (B68):
- (a) Include payments to policyholders of other groups arising from contract terms (whether to current or future policyholders)
- (b) Exclude payments already included in another group's fulfilment cash flows

**Worked example (B69):** If payments in one group are reduced from CU350 to CU250 because of guaranteed amounts to another group, the first group's fulfilment cash flows include the CU100 payment (i.e., CU350), and the second group's fulfilment cash flows exclude CU100 of the guaranteed amount.

**Practical approaches (B70):** If changes in underlying items and resulting cash flow effects can only be identified at a higher level of aggregation than the groups, allocate the effect to each group on a systematic and rational basis.

**Post-service fulfilment cash flows (B71):** After all services have been provided to contracts in a group, fulfilment cash flows may still include expected payments to current policyholders of other groups or future policyholders. Entity is not required to allocate such fulfilment cash flows to specific groups — may instead recognise a liability for all groups combined.

### Discount Rates

#### Which rates apply where (B72):
| Purpose | Rate |
|---------|------|
| Fulfilment cash flows | Current rates (paragraph 36) |
| CSM interest accretion (without DPF) | Locked-in rates at initial recognition |
| CSM adjustments for changes in FCF (without DPF) | Locked-in rates at initial recognition |
| PAA financing component | Locked-in rates at initial recognition |
| P&L disaggregation (no substantial financial risk effect) | Locked-in rates at initial recognition |
| P&L disaggregation (substantial financial risk effect) | Constant rate allocating remaining expected finance income/expenses |
| P&L disaggregation (PAA incurred claims) | Rate at date of incurred claim |

#### Principles (B74–B85):
- No double counting: cash flows not varying with underlying items → rates not reflecting variability (B74)
- Cash flows varying with financial underlying items → rates reflecting variability or adjusted + rate reflecting adjustment (B74(b))
- **Variability from contractual terms (B75):** Cash flows varying based on returns on financial underlying items shall be: (i) discounted using rates that reflect that variability, or (ii) adjusted for the effect of variability and discounted at a rate reflecting the adjustment. This includes variability from contractual terms, from the exercise of discretion, or from both.
- Nominal cash flows → nominal rates; real cash flows → real rates (B74(c)–(d))
- Guarantee floors: adjust rate even when guaranteed amount < expected return (B76)
- Not required to separate cash flows by variability; may use stochastic modelling for all (B77)

#### Estimation technique requirements (B78):
When observable market rates are not directly available, entity shall estimate appropriate rates. No particular technique prescribed, but entity shall:
- (a) Maximise use of observable inputs; non-market variables shall not contradict observable market variables
- (b) Reflect current market conditions from a market participant perspective
- (c) Exercise judgement on similarity between insurance contracts and instruments with observable prices, and adjust for differences

#### Two approaches to determine rates (B79–B85):
- **Bottom-up**: Start with liquid risk-free yield curve → adjust for illiquidity of insurance contracts
- **Top-down**: Start with yield curve from reference portfolio of assets → eliminate credit risk and other irrelevant factors

**Top-down estimation details (B82–B83):**
- Active market: use observable prices (consistent with IFRS 13 paragraph 69)
- Inactive market: adjust observable prices for similar assets (IFRS 13 paragraph 83)
- No market: use estimation technique with best available information, entity may place more weight on long-term estimates than short-term fluctuations (IFRS 13 paragraph 89)
- Adjust for: differences in amount/timing/uncertainty between reference portfolio and insurance contracts; exclude credit risk premiums (B83)

Both should produce similar results in theory; in practice may differ due to estimation limitations (B84).

**Reference portfolio selection (B85):** No restrictions on which assets to use, but fewer adjustments needed when characteristics are similar. Debt instruments generally require fewer adjustments than equity instruments for non-variable cash flows. For debt instruments, eliminate credit risk effect (e.g., using credit derivative market price as reference).

### Risk Adjustment for Non-Financial Risk

#### What it covers (B86):
Insurance risk + other non-financial risks (lapse risk, expense risk).

#### Conceptual basis (B87):
Compensation making entity indifferent between: liability with range of outcomes from non-financial risk vs. fixed liability with same expected PV.

#### Diversification and risk aversion (B88–B89):
The risk adjustment reflects:
- (a) The degree of **diversification benefit** the entity includes when determining required compensation
- (b) Both favourable and unfavourable outcomes, reflecting the entity's degree of **risk aversion**

The risk adjustment measures uncertainty from non-financial risk in insurance contracts. It shall NOT reflect risks that do not arise from insurance contracts, such as general operational risk (B89).

#### Characteristics (B91):
- (a) Low frequency/high severity → higher adjustment
- (b) Longer duration → higher adjustment
- (c) Wider probability distribution → higher adjustment
- (d) Less known about estimate and trend → higher adjustment
- (e) Emerging experience reducing uncertainty → decreasing adjustment

#### No prescribed technique (B92):
Must provide concise, informative disclosure for benchmarking. If not using confidence level technique, must disclose equivalent confidence level.

Included explicitly, conceptually separate from cash flow estimates and discount rates — no double counting (B90).

### Initial Recognition of Transfers

#### Transfers and business combinations (B93–B95F):
- Apply paragraphs 14–24 to identify groups as if entered at transaction date (B93)
- Use consideration received/paid as proxy for premiums (B94)
- In business combination: consideration = fair value (IFRS 3), not applying IFRS 13 paragraph 47 demand features
- Onerous contracts: excess of fulfilment cash flows over consideration → goodwill/bargain purchase gain (business combination) or loss in P&L (transfer) (B95A)

#### Reinsurance loss-recovery component in business combinations (B95B–B95D):
- At transaction date, determine loss-recovery component = loss component of underlying insurance contracts at that date × percentage of claims expected to recover from reinsurance (B95B)
- Recognise the loss-recovery component as part of goodwill or bargain purchase gain (business combination) or as income in P&L (transfer) (B95C)
- If onerous group includes contracts both covered and not covered by reinsurance, use systematic and rational allocation to determine the portion of the loss component relating to covered contracts (B95D)

#### Insurance acquisition cash flow assets in business combinations (B95E–B95F):
- Recognise asset for insurance acquisition cash flows at fair value at transaction date for rights to obtain: (a) future renewals of contracts recognised at transaction date, and (b) future contracts without repaying acquisition cash flows already paid by acquiree (B95F)
- The acquisition cash flow asset amount shall not be included in the measurement of acquired insurance contract groups (B95F)

### CSM Changes — Without Direct Participation Features

#### Adjustments to CSM (B96):
- (a) Experience adjustments from premiums relating to future service (at locked-in rates)
- (b) Changes in estimates of PV of future cash flows in liability for remaining coverage (at locked-in rates)
- (c) Differences between expected and actual investment component payments
- (ca) Differences between expected and actual policyholder loan repayments
- (d) Changes in risk adjustment for non-financial risk relating to future service

#### Do NOT adjust CSM for (B97):
- (a) Time value of money/financial risk effects (on cash flows, risk adjustment, discount rates)
- (b) Changes in estimates for liability for incurred claims
- (c) Experience adjustments not relating to future service

#### Discretionary cash flows (B98–B100):
Entity specifies at inception the basis for its commitment. Changes in discretionary cash flows = future service → adjust CSM. Changes in financial risk assumptions on commitment ≠ future service → don't adjust CSM.

### CSM Changes — With Direct Participation Features

#### Definition criteria (B101):
At inception: (a) policyholder participates in clearly identified pool of underlying items, (b) entity expects to pay substantial share of fair value returns, (c) entity expects substantial proportion of changes to policyholder to vary with fair value of underlying items.

Assessed at inception only (B102). Reinsurance contracts cannot be DPF contracts (B109).

#### Obligation structure (B104):
Obligation to policyholder = fair value of underlying items MINUS variable fee (entity's share of fair value of underlying items LESS fulfilment cash flows not varying with underlying items)

#### CSM adjustments (B110–B114):
- Changes in obligation to pay fair value of underlying items → NOT future service, don't adjust CSM (B111)
- Changes in entity's share of fair value → future service, adjust CSM (B112)
- Changes in non-varying fulfilment cash flows → apply B96–B97 consistently with general model; changes in financial guarantees/time value relate to future service (B113)
- May determine combined amount for B112+B113 adjustments (B114)

#### Risk mitigation (B115–B118):
- Entity may choose not to recognise CSM changes from financial risk effects on: (a) entity's share of underlying items (if mitigated by derivatives/reinsurance), (b) fulfilment cash flows from financial guarantees (if mitigated by derivatives, non-derivative FVTPL instruments, or reinsurance)
- Conditions (B116): documented risk management objective/strategy, economic offset exists, credit risk doesn't dominate
- If mitigated by derivatives/FVTPL instruments: include related insurance finance I/E in P&L (B117A)
- Cease applying if conditions no longer met; no adjustment for prior P&L amounts (B118)

### Coverage Units

#### Determining amount to recognise in P&L (B119):
1. Identify coverage units — quantity of insurance contract services, considering quantity of benefits and expected coverage period
2. Allocate CSM at end of period equally to each coverage unit provided and expected
3. Recognise amount allocated to current period units

**IFRIC Agenda Decision (June 2022) — Annuity contracts:**
For annuity contracts, Method 1 (based on amount policyholder can validly claim in the period) meets B119 principle. Method 2 (based on PV of expected future annuity payments) does NOT — it assigns benefits to periods with no insured event and misrepresents quantity of benefits by including future-period amounts.

#### Investment-return service period (B119A):
Ends at or before date all amounts due to current policyholders have been paid.

#### Investment-return service for non-DPF contracts (B119B):
Exists if and only if: (a) investment component exists or policyholder has withdrawal right, (b) expected investment return (may be below zero), (c) entity expects to perform investment activity to generate that return.

### Reinsurance Loss Recovery

#### When it applies (B119C):
Reinsurance contract entered into before or at same time as onerous underlying contracts are recognised.

#### Calculating the adjustment (B119D):
Loss on underlying contracts × percentage of claims expected to recover from reinsurance.

#### Mixed groups (B119E):
If onerous group includes both covered and uncovered contracts, use systematic and rational allocation to determine the portion of losses relating to covered contracts.

#### Loss-recovery component tracking (B119F):
Adjust to reflect changes in loss component of underlying contracts. Carrying amount of loss-recovery component shall not exceed portion of loss component entity expects to recover.

## Insurance Revenue

### Total consideration (B120–B121):
Premiums adjusted for financing effect, excluding investment components. Comprises:
- (a) Service-related: insurance service expenses + risk adjustment + CSM
- (b) Insurance acquisition cash flow recovery

### Revenue recognition principle (B123):
Revenue = reduction in liability for remaining coverage from services provided, excluding:
- (a) Changes not from services: premium receipts, investment components, policyholder loans, transaction taxes, insurance finance I/E, acquisition cash flows, derecognition of transferred liabilities
- (b) Changes for services without expected consideration: loss component increases/decreases

### Derecognition of pre-recognition assets (B123A):
To the extent an entity derecognises an asset for cash flows other than insurance acquisition cash flows at initial recognition (paragraphs 38(c)(ii) and B66A), it shall recognise insurance revenue and expenses for the amount derecognised at that date.

### Revenue analysis (B124):
Revenue = (a) insurance service expenses incurred (expected at period start, excluding loss component allocation, investment component repayments, taxes, acquisition expenses, risk adjustment amounts) + (b) change in risk adjustment (excluding insurance finance, CSM adjustments, loss component allocations) + (c) CSM recognised + (d) other amounts (experience adjustments for premiums not relating to future service)

### Acquisition cash flow recovery in revenue (B125):
Allocate portion of premiums recovering acquisition cash flows to each period systematically on passage of time basis. Recognise same amount as insurance service expenses.

### Change of PAA allocation basis (B127):
Entity shall change allocation basis between passage of time (B126(a)) and expected timing of incurred expenses (B126(b)) as necessary if facts and circumstances change.

### PAA insurance revenue (B126):
Expected premium receipts (excluding investment components, adjusted for time value of money if applicable) allocated to each period:
- (a) On passage of time basis; BUT
- (b) On basis of expected timing of incurred expenses if pattern differs significantly from passage of time

## Insurance Finance Income or Expenses

### What it comprises (B128):
- (a) Inflation assumptions based on price index/inflation-linked assets = financial risk
- (b) Inflation assumptions based on entity-specific price expectations ≠ financial risk
- (c) Changes from underlying item value changes (excluding additions/withdrawals) = financial risk

### OCI disaggregation (B129–B136):

**Accounting policy choice applied per portfolio**, considering assets held and their accounting (B129).

**Systematic allocation for P&L amount (B130):**
- Based on contract characteristics, not asset returns
- OCI cumulative amounts = zero over group duration

**For contracts with no substantial financial risk effect on policyholder payments (B131):**
Use locked-in discount rates at initial recognition.

**For contracts with substantial financial risk effect (B132):**
- Future cash flows: (i) constant rate over remaining duration, or (ii) crediting-rate-based allocation
- Risk adjustment (if separately disaggregated): consistent allocation
- CSM: (i) locked-in rates for non-DPF, (ii) consistent allocation for DPF

**PAA incurred claims (B133):**
Use discount rates at date of incurred claim.

**DPF contracts where entity holds underlying items (B134):**
Include in P&L expenses/income exactly matching underlying items income/expenses (net = nil).

**Change between paragraph 88 and 89 policies (B135–B136):**
When entity starts/stops holding underlying items: reclassify accumulated OCI to P&L in period of change and future periods (no restatement of prior periods, no recalculation of accumulated OCI).
