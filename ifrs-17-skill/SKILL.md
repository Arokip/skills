# IFRS 17 Insurance Contracts — Complete Standard Reference

You are an expert on IFRS 17 Insurance Contracts. This skill contains the complete text of the standard as issued by the IASB (May 2017, amended June 2020 and December 2021). Use it to provide accurate, authoritative answers grounded in the actual paragraphs of the standard.

## Objective (Paragraph 1)

IFRS 17 establishes principles for the recognition, measurement, presentation and disclosure of insurance contracts. The objective is to ensure that an entity provides relevant information that faithfully represents those contracts, giving users of financial statements a basis to assess the effect that insurance contracts have on the entity's financial position, financial performance and cash flows.

**Key principles** (from IN6 of the Introduction):
- (a) An entity identifies its insurance contracts
- (b) Separates specified embedded derivatives, distinct investment components, and distinct performance obligations
- (c) Divides contracts into groups and measures them at: (i) a risk-adjusted present value of future cash flows; plus/minus (ii) the contractual service margin (unearned profit)
- (d) Recognises profit over the coverage period as the entity provides services and is released from risk; recognises losses immediately for onerous groups
- (e) Presents separately: insurance revenue, insurance service expenses, and insurance finance income or expenses

An entity shall consider its substantive rights and obligations, whether from contract, law or regulation (paragraph 2). Contracts can be written, oral or implied.

## Scope (Paragraphs 3–8A)

### What IFRS 17 applies to (paragraph 3):
- (a) Insurance contracts issued (including reinsurance contracts issued)
- (b) Reinsurance contracts held
- (c) Investment contracts with discretionary participation features (if entity also issues insurance contracts)

### What IFRS 17 does NOT apply to (paragraph 7):
- (a) Manufacturer/dealer/retailer warranties (→ IFRS 15)
- (b) Employee benefit plan assets/liabilities (→ IAS 19, IFRS 2, IAS 26)
- (c) Contractual rights/obligations contingent on future use of non-financial items
- (d) Residual value guarantees by manufacturers/dealers/retailers and lessees
- (e) Financial guarantee contracts (unless issuer previously treated them as insurance — then choose IFRS 17 or IAS 32/IFRS 7/IFRS 9, irrevocable per contract)
- (f) Contingent consideration in business combinations (→ IFRS 3)
- (g) Insurance contracts where entity is policyholder (except reinsurance held)
- (h) Credit card contracts meeting insurance definition IF insurance risk not reflected in individual pricing (→ IFRS 9)

### Fixed-fee service contracts (paragraph 8):
Contracts primarily providing services for a fixed fee may apply IFRS 15 instead if:
- (a) No individual risk assessment in pricing
- (b) Compensation by providing services, not cash
- (c) Insurance risk primarily from customer's use of services

Choice is contract-by-contract, irrevocable.

### Death waiver loans and similar contracts (paragraph 8A):
Contracts meeting the insurance definition but limiting compensation to the amount required to settle the policyholder's obligation created by the contract (e.g., loans with death waivers): entity shall choose to apply IFRS 17 or IFRS 9. Choice is per **portfolio** of insurance contracts, irrevocable.

### Combination of contracts (paragraph 9):
A set/series with the same counterparty may need to be treated as a whole if they achieve an overall commercial effect.

### Separating components (paragraphs 10–13):
1. **Embedded derivatives**: Apply IFRS 9 to determine if separation needed (paragraph 11(a))
2. **Distinct investment components**: Separate if (i) not highly interrelated with insurance component AND (ii) equivalent contract sold separately in same market (paragraph 11(b)). Apply IFRS 9 to separated component.
3. **Distinct goods/services other than insurance**: Apply IFRS 15 separation (paragraph 12). Attribute cash inflows using IFRS 15; attribute cash outflows directly where possible, remainder on systematic and rational basis.
4. All remaining components: Apply IFRS 17 (paragraph 13)

## Level of Aggregation (Paragraphs 14–24)

### Portfolios (paragraph 14):
A portfolio comprises contracts subject to **similar risks** and **managed together**. Same product line = similar risks; different product lines = different portfolios.

**IFRIC Agenda Decision (September 2022) — Multi-currency groups:** "Similar risks" in paragraph 14 refers to ALL risks, including currency exchange rate risks (a financial risk). However, "similar" does not mean "identical" — a portfolio may include contracts with different currency exchange rate risks. What an entity considers similar depends on the nature and extent of risks in its contracts. For multi-currency groups, the entity applies IAS 21 to translate the carrying amount (including CSM) at the closing rate, and uses judgement to develop an accounting policy on currency denomination (single-currency or multi-currency). The entity cannot simply presume the CSM is denominated in the functional currency.

### Groups within portfolios — minimum three groups (paragraph 16):
- (a) **Onerous at initial recognition**
- (b) **No significant possibility of becoming onerous subsequently**
- (c) **Remaining contracts** in the portfolio

### Annual cohorts (paragraph 22):
An entity shall NOT include contracts issued more than **one year apart** in the same group.

### PAA simplification (paragraph 18):
For PAA contracts, assume no contracts are onerous at initial recognition unless facts indicate otherwise.

### Assessment of profitability (paragraph 19):
For non-PAA contracts, assess based on (a) likelihood of changes in assumptions, and (b) information from internal reporting.

### Regulatory constraints (paragraph 20):
If contracts would fall into different groups only because regulation constrains pricing, they may be in the same group. Do not apply by analogy.

### Permitted subdivisions (paragraph 21):
May further divide into more profitability groups or more onerous groups.

### Single contract groups (paragraph 23):
A group may comprise a single contract if that results from applying paragraphs 14–22.

### No reassessment (paragraph 24):
Groups are established at initial recognition and not reassessed. May estimate fulfilment cash flows at higher aggregation level and allocate down.

## Recognition (Paragraphs 25–28F)

### When to recognise (paragraph 25):
Recognise a group from the **earliest** of:
- (a) Beginning of the coverage period
- (b) Date when first payment from policyholder becomes due
- (c) For onerous groups: when the group becomes onerous

### Adding contracts to groups (paragraph 28):
Add contracts in the reporting period when they meet paragraph 25 criteria. May result in revised discount rates from start of that reporting period.

### Insurance acquisition cash flows (paragraphs 28A–28F):
- Allocate to groups using systematic and rational method (28A)
- Recognise as asset before related group is recognised (28B)
- Derecognise when included in group measurement (28C)
- Assess recoverability at each reporting period end if impairment indicators exist (28E)
- Recognise impairment loss reversal when conditions improve (28F)

## Measurement (Paragraphs 29–71)

### Three measurement models:

| Model | Applies to | Key reference |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| **General Model (BBA)** | Default for all insurance contracts | Paragraphs 32–52 |
| **Premium Allocation Approach (PAA)** | Simplified; eligible groups | Paragraphs 53–59 |
| **Variable Fee Approach (VFA)** | Direct participation features | Paragraph 45 (modifies General Model) |

### Non-performance risk (paragraph 31):
Fulfilment cash flows shall NOT reflect the non-performance risk of the issuing entity.

### Foreign currency (paragraph 30):
Treat the group (including CSM) as a **monetary item** under IAS 21.

---

### Initial Recognition — General Model (Paragraph 32)

Measure at the total of:

**(a) Fulfilment cash flows:**
1. **Estimates of future cash flows** (paragraphs 33–35)
   - Include all future cash flows within the contract boundary
   - Probability-weighted mean (expected value) of full range of outcomes
   - Unbiased, entity perspective (consistent with market for market variables), current, explicit

2. **Discount adjustment** (paragraph 36) — for time value of money and financial risks:
   - Reflect time value of money, cash flow characteristics, liquidity characteristics
   - Consistent with observable market prices
   - Exclude factors not affecting insurance contract cash flows

3. **Risk adjustment for non-financial risk** (paragraph 37):
   - Compensation entity requires for bearing uncertainty from non-financial risk

**(b) Contractual service margin (CSM)** (paragraphs 38–39):
- Represents **unearned profit**
- Set so that no income or expenses arise on initial recognition (calibration amount)
- Absorbs: (i) fulfilment cash flows, (ii) cash flows at that date, (iii) derecognition of insurance acquisition cash flow assets and other pre-recognition assets/liabilities

### Contract boundary (paragraph 34):
Cash flows are within the boundary during the period the entity can compel premium payment or has a substantive obligation to provide services. Substantive obligation ends when entity can:
- (a) Reassess risks of particular policyholder and fully reprice; OR
- (b) Reassess portfolio risk and fully reprice, AND premiums don't account for post-reassessment risks

### Subsequent Measurement (Paragraphs 40–46)

**Carrying amount = Liability for remaining coverage + Liability for incurred claims**

**Liability for remaining coverage** (paragraph 40(a)):
- (i) Fulfilment cash flows for future service
- (ii) CSM

**Liability for incurred claims** (paragraph 40(b)):
- Fulfilment cash flows for past service

### Income and expenses from remaining coverage (paragraph 41):
- (a) **Insurance revenue** — reduction for services provided (paragraphs B120–B124)
- (b) **Insurance service expenses** — losses on onerous groups and reversals
- (c) **Insurance finance income/expenses** — time value of money and financial risk

### Income and expenses from incurred claims (paragraph 42):
- (a) **Insurance service expenses** — increase in liability for claims and expenses incurred in the period (excluding investment components)
- (b) **Insurance service expenses** — subsequent changes in fulfilment cash flows relating to incurred claims and incurred expenses
- (c) **Insurance finance income/expenses** — effect of time value of money and financial risk (paragraph 87)

### CSM roll-forward for contracts WITHOUT direct participation features (paragraph 44):
Adjusted for:
- (a) New contracts added
- (b) Interest accretion at locked-in discount rates
- (c) Changes in fulfilment cash flows relating to future service (except where exceeding CSM → loss, or allocated to loss component)
- (d) Currency exchange differences
- (e) Amount recognised as insurance revenue (allocated over coverage period using coverage units — paragraph B119)

### CSM roll-forward for contracts WITH direct participation features (paragraph 45):
Adjusted for:
- (a) New contracts added
- (b) Change in entity's share of fair value of underlying items
- (c) Changes in fulfilment cash flows relating to future service
- (d) Currency exchange differences
- (e) Amount recognised as insurance revenue
- Adjustments (b)–(c) subject to risk mitigation option (B115) and loss/loss component limits

### Onerous Contracts (Paragraphs 47–52)

**At initial recognition (paragraph 47):**
If fulfilment cash flows + acquisition cash flows + cash flows at that date = net outflow → contract is onerous. Recognise loss immediately; CSM = zero.

**Subsequent measurement (paragraph 48):**
Group becomes (more) onerous when unfavourable changes in fulfilment cash flows relating to future service exceed CSM carrying amount. Recognise loss for the excess.

**Loss component (paragraph 49):**
Establish/increase a loss component of the liability for remaining coverage depicting losses. Determines amounts presented as reversals of losses (excluded from insurance revenue).

**Allocation after loss (paragraph 50):**
- (a) Allocate subsequent fulfilment cash flow changes systematically between loss component and rest of liability for remaining coverage
- (b) Allocate solely to loss component until zero: (i) favourable changes in future service estimates, (ii) increases in entity's share of underlying items

**Loss component reaches zero (paragraph 52):**
By end of coverage period, total amounts allocated to loss component = zero.

### Premium Allocation Approach (Paragraphs 53–59)

**Eligibility (paragraph 53):**
- (a) Measurement would not differ materially from general model; OR
- (b) Coverage period of each contract ≤ one year

**Not met if (paragraph 54):** Significant variability in fulfilment cash flows expected (e.g., embedded derivatives, long coverage periods).

**Liability for remaining coverage (paragraph 55):**
- Initial (55(a)): premiums received − acquisition cash flows (unless expensed per 59(a)) ± derecognition of pre-recognition assets/liabilities (acquisition cash flow assets per 28C and other assets/liabilities per B66A)
- Subsequently (55(b)): opening balance + (i) premiums received − (ii) acquisition cash flows (unless expensed per 59(a)) + (iii) amortisation of previously-expensed acquisition cash flows (unless expensed per 59(a)) + (iv) financing component (paragraph 56) − (v) insurance revenue for services provided (paragraph B126) − (vi) investment components paid/transferred to incurred claims

**Financing component (paragraph 56):**
Adjust for time value of money if significant financing component (not required if time between service and premium due ≤ 1 year).

**Onerous contracts test (paragraphs 57–58):**
Compare carrying amount of liability for remaining coverage with fulfilment cash flows for remaining coverage. Recognise loss for any excess.

**Simplifications (paragraph 59):**
- (a) May expense acquisition cash flows if coverage ≤ 1 year
- (b) Measure liability for incurred claims at fulfilment cash flows (no discounting required if expected payment ≤ 1 year from claim incurrence)

### Reinsurance Contracts Held (Paragraphs 60–70A)

**Level of aggregation (paragraph 61):** Apply paragraphs 14–24, replacing "onerous" with "net gain on initial recognition."

**Recognition (paragraph 62):** Earlier of:
- (a) Beginning of coverage period
- (b) Date entity recognises an onerous group of underlying contracts (if reinsurance entered into at/before that date)

**Proportionate coverage delay (paragraph 62A):** Delay recognition of proportionate-coverage reinsurance held until the date any underlying insurance contract is initially recognised, if that date is later than the beginning of the reinsurance coverage period.

**Measurement (paragraph 63):** Use consistent assumptions with underlying contracts. Include risk of non-performance by reinsurer.

**Risk adjustment (paragraph 64):** Represents risk being transferred to reinsurer.

**CSM (paragraph 65):** Net cost or net gain on purchasing reinsurance, measured as the sum of: (a) fulfilment cash flows, (b) amount derecognised for previously recognised assets/liabilities for cash flows related to the group, (c) cash flows arising at that date, and (d) income recognised applying paragraph 66A. Unless paragraph 65A applies.

**Retroactive reinsurance cost (paragraph 65A):** If the net cost of purchasing reinsurance relates to events that occurred **before** the purchase, recognise that cost immediately in profit or loss as an expense (notwithstanding paragraph B5).

**CSM roll-forward (paragraph 66):** Similar to general model but includes loss-recovery income (66A) and loss-recovery component (66B).

**Loss recovery (paragraphs 66A–66B):** When entity recognises loss on onerous underlying contracts, adjust reinsurance CSM and recognise income. Establish loss-recovery component of the reinsurance asset.

**Non-performance risk and CSM (paragraph 67):** Changes in fulfilment cash flows resulting from changes in the risk of non-performance by the reinsurer do NOT relate to future service and shall NOT adjust the CSM.

**Cannot be onerous (paragraph 68):** Paragraphs 47–52 do not apply to reinsurance held.

**PAA for reinsurance (paragraphs 69–70A):** Same eligibility criteria, adapted for reinsurance features.

### Investment Contracts with Discretionary Participation Features (Paragraph 71)

Modified requirements:
- (a) Initial recognition date = when entity becomes party to contract
- (b) Contract boundary: cash flows from substantive obligation to deliver cash
- (c) CSM allocation: systematic basis reflecting transfer of investment services

## Modification and Derecognition (Paragraphs 72–77)

### Modification (paragraph 72):
Derecognise original and recognise modified contract as new if any of:
- (a) Modified terms would have: (i) excluded from IFRS 17 scope, (ii) different separated components, (iii) substantially different contract boundary, (iv) different group
- (b) Was/became direct participation vs. not
- (c) Was/became ineligible for PAA

Otherwise, treat as changes in estimates of fulfilment cash flows (paragraph 73).

### Derecognition (paragraph 74):
Only when obligation expires, is discharged, or cancelled; or modification conditions met.

## Presentation (Paragraphs 78–92)

### Statement of Financial Position (paragraphs 78–79):
Present separately portfolios of:
- (a) Insurance contracts issued — assets
- (b) Insurance contracts issued — liabilities
- (c) Reinsurance contracts held — assets
- (d) Reinsurance contracts held — liabilities

### Statement(s) of Financial Performance (paragraph 80):
Disaggregate into:
- **(a) Insurance service result** = insurance revenue + insurance service expenses
- **(b) Insurance finance income or expenses**

### Risk adjustment disaggregation (paragraph 81):
Entity is NOT required to disaggregate the change in risk adjustment for non-financial risk between insurance service result and insurance finance income/expenses. If no disaggregation, the entire change is included in the insurance service result.

### Reinsurance (paragraph 82):
Present income/expenses from reinsurance held separately from insurance contracts issued.

### Insurance Revenue (paragraph 83):
Depicts provision of services at amount reflecting expected consideration. Excludes investment components (paragraph 85).

### Insurance Service Expenses (paragraph 84):
Incurred claims (excluding investment component repayments), other incurred insurance service expenses, and other amounts per paragraph 103(b).

### Reinsurance presentation options (paragraph 86):
Entity may present reinsurance income/expenses (other than insurance finance I/E) as a single net amount; OR separately show amounts recovered from reinsurer and allocation of premiums paid. If presenting separately:
- (a) Reinsurance cash flows contingent on underlying claims → part of expected reimbursement claims
- (b) Non-contingent amounts from reinsurer (e.g., ceding commissions) → reduction in premiums paid to reinsurer
- (ba) Amounts from loss recovery (paragraphs 66(c)(i)–(ii) and 66A–66B) → amounts recovered from reinsurer
- (c) Do NOT present allocation of premiums paid as reduction in revenue

### Insurance Finance Income or Expenses (paragraph 87):
Change in carrying amount arising from:
- (a) Effect of time value of money and changes therein
- (b) Effect of financial risk and changes therein
- (c) Excluding changes for direct participation features that would adjust CSM but don't (→ insurance service expenses)

### Risk mitigation routing (paragraph 87A):
- (a) Insurance finance I/E arising from risk mitigation (paragraph B115) → apply B117A
- (b) All other insurance finance I/E → apply paragraphs 88 and 89

### OCI option (paragraphs 88–89):
**Accounting policy choice** (applied per portfolio):
- Include all in profit or loss; OR
- Disaggregate: systematic allocation to P&L, remainder in OCI

For direct participation features where entity holds underlying items:
- Include all in P&L; OR
- Disaggregate to eliminate accounting mismatches with underlying items in P&L

### OCI mechanics (paragraph 90):
If entity chooses disaggregation (88(b) or 89(b)), include in OCI the difference between the P&L amount and total insurance finance income/expenses for the period.

### Reclassification on derecognition/transfer (paragraph 91):
- (a) If entity chose 88(b): reclassify remaining OCI amounts to P&L as reclassification adjustment
- (b) If entity chose 89(b): do NOT reclassify remaining OCI amounts to P&L

### Foreign exchange differences (paragraph 92):
Include exchange differences on groups of insurance contracts in P&L, unless they relate to changes included in OCI applying paragraph 90 (in which case include in OCI).

## Disclosure (Paragraphs 93–132)

### Objective (paragraph 93):
Disclose information enabling users to assess effect of insurance contracts on financial position, performance and cash flows:
- (a) Amounts recognised (paragraphs 97–116)
- (b) Significant judgements (paragraphs 117–120)
- (c) Nature and extent of risks (paragraphs 121–132)

### Key disclosure requirements:

**PAA-specific disclosures (paragraph 97):**
Only paragraphs 98–100, 102–103, 105–105B, and 109A apply to PAA contracts. Additionally disclose: (a) which eligibility criteria met (paragraphs 53/69), (b) whether time value adjustments are made (paragraphs 56, 57(b), 59(b)), (c) acquisition cash flow recognition method chosen (paragraph 59(a)).

**Reconciliations (paragraphs 98–105B):**
- Opening to closing balances for: remaining coverage (excluding loss component), loss component, incurred claims
- For non-PAA: also reconcile fulfilment cash flows PV, risk adjustment, CSM
- Separate for insurance contracts issued and reinsurance held
- Include: insurance revenue, insurance service expenses (incurred claims, acquisition cash flow amortisation, past/future service changes), cash flows, insurance finance income/expenses

**Insurance revenue analysis (paragraph 106):**
For non-PAA contracts: insurance service expenses incurred, change in risk adjustment, CSM recognised, other amounts, plus acquisition cash flow recovery allocation.

**Effect of new contracts (paragraph 107):**
Disclose at initial recognition: PV future cash outflows, PV future cash inflows, risk adjustment, CSM.

**Insurance acquisition cash flow asset reconciliation (paragraphs 105A–105B):**
Disclose reconciliation from opening to closing balance of assets for insurance acquisition cash flows (paragraph 28B), including impairment losses and reversals (paragraphs 28E–28F), aggregated consistently with insurance contract reconciliations.

**Effect of new contracts at initial recognition (paragraphs 107–108):**
For non-PAA contracts, disclose: (a) PV of future cash outflows (showing acquisition cash flows separately), (b) PV of future cash inflows, (c) risk adjustment, (d) CSM. Separately disclose amounts from transfers/business combinations and from onerous groups.

**CSM run-off (paragraph 109):**
When entity expects to recognise remaining CSM in P&L, in time bands. Separately for insurance contracts issued and reinsurance held.

**Acquisition cash flow asset run-off (paragraph 109A):**
Disclose quantitatively, in appropriate time bands, when entity expects to derecognise acquisition cash flow assets (paragraph 28C).

**Insurance finance income or expenses disclosures (paragraphs 110–113):**
- Disclose and explain total insurance finance I/E, including relationship with investment return on assets (110)
- For DPF contracts: describe composition and fair value of underlying items (111)
- For DPF contracts using B115 risk mitigation option: disclose effect on CSM adjustment (112)
- For DPF contracts changing disaggregation basis (B135): disclose reason, adjustment amounts per line item, and carrying amount of group at change date (113)

**Transition amount disclosures (paragraphs 114–116):**
Disclose CSM reconciliation and insurance revenue separately for: (a) modified retrospective approach contracts, (b) fair value approach contracts, (c) all other contracts. Explain how transition measurements were determined (115). If disaggregating insurance finance I/E, disclose reconciliation of cumulative OCI amounts for related financial assets at FVOCI (116).

**Regulatory framework (paragraph 126):**
Disclose the effect of regulatory frameworks (e.g., minimum capital requirements, interest-rate guarantees). If applying paragraph 20 (regulatory pricing constraints), disclose that fact.

**Significant judgements (paragraphs 117–120):**
- Methods, inputs, assumptions, estimation techniques
- Approach to: discretionary cash flows, risk adjustment, discount rates, investment components, coverage unit weighting
- Confidence level for risk adjustment (or equivalent)
- Yield curve(s) used for discounting

**Risk disclosures (paragraphs 121–132):**
- Concentrations of risk (paragraph 127)
- Sensitivity analysis for insurance and market risks (paragraphs 128–129)
- Claims development — up to 10 years (paragraph 130)
- Credit risk — maximum exposure, credit quality of reinsurance assets (paragraph 131)
- Liquidity risk — maturity analysis for first 5 years and aggregate beyond (paragraph 132)

## Defined Terms (Appendix A)

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Contractual service margin (CSM)** | Component of carrying amount representing unearned profit to be recognised as entity provides insurance contract services |
| **Coverage period** | Period during which entity provides insurance contract services. This period includes the insurance contract services that relate to all premiums within the boundary of the insurance contract |
| **Experience adjustment** | Difference between estimate at period start of expected amounts and actual amounts (for premiums/claims) |
| **Financial risk** | Risk of possible future change in interest rate, financial instrument price, commodity price, FX rate, index, credit rating/index, or other non-entity-specific variable |
| **Fulfilment cash flows** | Explicit, unbiased, probability-weighted estimate (expected value) of PV of future cash outflows minus inflows, including risk adjustment for non-financial risk |
| **Group of insurance contracts** | Set from dividing a portfolio into minimum: onerous, no significant possibility of becoming onerous, remaining — issued within one year |
| **Insurance acquisition cash flows** | Cash flows from costs of selling, underwriting and starting a group, directly attributable to the portfolio |
| **Insurance contract** | Contract where issuer accepts significant insurance risk from policyholder by agreeing to compensate for specified uncertain future event (insured event) adversely affecting policyholder |
| **Insurance contract services** | (a) Insurance coverage, (b) investment-return service (without DPF), (c) investment-related service (with DPF) |
| **Insurance contract with direct participation features** | At inception: (a) policyholder participates in clearly identified pool of underlying items, (b) entity expects to pay substantial share of fair value returns, (c) substantial proportion of changes to policyholder varies with fair value of underlying items |
| **Insurance contract without direct participation features** | Not a contract with direct participation features |
| **Insurance risk** | Risk, other than financial risk, transferred from contract holder to issuer |
| **Insured event** | Uncertain future event covered by insurance contract that creates insurance risk |
| **Investment component** | Amounts the contract requires entity to repay to policyholder in all circumstances, regardless of insured event occurrence |
| **Investment contract with DPF** | Financial instrument providing contractual right to receive, supplementing a non-discretionary amount, additional amounts that are: significant portion of total benefits, at issuer's discretion for timing/amount, based on specified pool/asset returns/entity profit |
| **Liability for incurred claims** | Obligation to: (a) investigate and pay valid claims for occurred events (including IBNR), (b) pay related amounts for past service or investment components not in remaining coverage |
| **Liability for remaining coverage** | Obligation to: (a) investigate and pay claims for not-yet-occurred events, (b) pay amounts for future service or investment components not yet transferred to incurred claims |
| **Policyholder** | Party with right to compensation if insured event occurs |
| **Portfolio of insurance contracts** | Contracts subject to similar risks and managed together |
| **Reinsurance contract** | Insurance contract issued by reinsurer to compensate another entity for claims from its underlying contracts |
| **Risk adjustment for non-financial risk** | Compensation entity requires for bearing uncertainty in amount/timing of cash flows from non-financial risk |
| **Underlying items** | Items determining some amounts payable to policyholder (e.g., reference portfolio of assets, net assets of entity) |

## Quick Decision Trees

### Which measurement model?

```
Is coverage period ≤ 1 year, or would PAA not differ materially from BBA?
├── YES → Premium Allocation Approach (PAA) permitted (paragraphs 53–59)
└── NO
    Does the contract have direct participation features? (paragraphs B101–B109)
    ├── YES → Variable Fee Approach (VFA) — General Model modified by paragraph 45
    └── NO → General Model / Building Blocks Approach (BBA) (paragraphs 32–52)
```

### Is it an insurance contract?

```
Does the contract transfer significant insurance risk? (paragraphs B17–B23)
├── NO → Not an insurance contract (apply IFRS 9, IFRS 15, etc.)
└── YES
    Is it excluded by paragraph 7?
    ├── YES → Apply other standard (IFRS 15, IAS 19, etc.)
    └── NO → Apply IFRS 17
```

### CSM adjustment or P&L?

```
Change in fulfilment cash flows:
├── Relates to future service → Adjust CSM (paragraph 44(c) / 45(b)-(c))
│   └── Exceeds CSM → Loss in P&L (paragraph 48)
├── Relates to current service → Insurance revenue / service expenses (paragraph 41)
├── Relates to past service → Insurance service expenses (paragraph 42(b))
└── Time value of money / financial risk → Insurance finance income/expenses (paragraph 87)
```
