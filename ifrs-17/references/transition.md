## Effective Date

### C1
Annual reporting periods beginning on or after **1 January 2023**. Early application permitted for entities that apply IFRS 9 on or before the date of initial application.

### C2
- **Date of initial application**: beginning of annual reporting period in which entity first applies IFRS 17
- **Transition date**: beginning of annual reporting period immediately preceding date of initial application

### C2A
Initial Application of IFRS 17 and IFRS 9—Comparative Information (December 2021) added paragraphs C28A–C28E and C33A. Applied on initial application of IFRS 17.

## Transition Overview

### Fully retrospective approach (C3–C4):
Unless impracticable or C5A applies, apply IFRS 17 **retrospectively**:
- (a) Identify, recognise and measure each group as if IFRS 17 had always applied
- (aa) Same for assets for insurance acquisition cash flows (no need to apply impairment test before transition)
- (b) Derecognise balances that would not exist under IFRS 17
- (c) Recognise net difference in equity

Exceptions: no need for IAS 8 paragraph 28(f) quantitative information; B115 risk mitigation option only prospective from transition date.

### If retrospective is impracticable (C5):
For each group, choose:
- (a) **Modified retrospective approach** (C6–C19A) — subject to reasonable and supportable information requirement; OR
- (b) **Fair value approach** (C20–C24B)

### Direct participation features with risk mitigation (C5A):
May choose fair value approach (even if retrospective is possible) if:
- (a) Choosing to apply B115 risk mitigation prospectively from transition date; AND
- (b) Used derivatives, FVTPL instruments, or reinsurance to mitigate financial risk before transition

### Insurance acquisition cash flows (C5B):
If retrospective is impracticable for acquisition cash flow assets:
- (a) Modified retrospective approach (C14B–C14D, C17A); OR
- (b) Fair value approach (C24A–C24B)

## Modified Retrospective Approach

### Objective (C6):
Achieve **closest outcome to retrospective** using reasonable and supportable information without undue cost or effort.
- Use information that would have been used for fully retrospective approach
- If insufficient information → apply fair value approach instead

### Permitted modifications (C7–C8):
Use each modification only to extent entity lacks reasonable and supportable information for retrospective.

### Assessments at inception/initial recognition (C9–C10):

May determine at transition date (C9):
- (a) How to identify groups (paragraphs 14–24)
- (b) Whether contract meets DPF definition (B101–B109)
- (c) How to identify discretionary cash flows (B98–B100)
- (d) Whether investment contract meets DPF definition (paragraph 71)

May classify settlement claims as liability for incurred claims (C9A).

May include contracts issued more than one year apart in same group (C10) — i.e., annual cohort requirement relaxed.

### CSM for contracts without direct participation features (C11–C16C):

**Estimate future cash flows at initial recognition (C12):**
Use future cash flows at transition date (or earlier determinable date), adjusted for cash flows known to have occurred since initial recognition (including from contracts that ceased to exist).

**Discount rates (C13):**
- (a) Use observable yield curve approximating paragraph 36 rates for at least 3 years before transition; OR
- (b) If no such curve: estimate using average spread between observable curve and paragraph 36 curve (average over at least 3 years before transition)

**Risk adjustment at initial recognition (C14):**
Adjust transition-date risk adjustment by expected release of risk before transition (by reference to similar contracts issued at transition date).

**Interim financial statements (C14A):**
May determine CSM/loss component as if no interim financial statements prepared before transition.

**Insurance acquisition cash flows (C14B–C14D):**
- Use same systematic and rational method expected after transition (C14B)
- Acquisition cash flows allocated to recognised groups adjust CSM; others recognised as asset (C14C)
- If no reasonable information: set adjustment to CSM and acquisition cash flow asset to nil at transition (C14D)

**CSM at transition from initial recognition CSM (C15):**
- (a) Accrete interest using C13 rates
- (b) Determine amount recognised for services before transition by comparing remaining coverage units at transition with units provided before transition

**Loss component at initial recognition (C16):**
Determine amounts allocated to loss component before transition using systematic basis.

**Reinsurance loss-recovery component (C16A–C16C):**
Determine at transition by multiplying: loss component of underlying contracts at transition × percentage of claims expected to recover. If mixed group, use systematic allocation (C16B). If no reasonable information, don't identify loss-recovery component (C16C).

### CSM for contracts with direct participation features (C17–C17A):

**CSM or loss component at transition (C17):**
= Fair value of underlying items at transition
− Fulfilment cash flows at transition
± Adjustment for: (i) amounts charged to policyholders before transition, (ii) amounts paid before transition not varying with underlying items, (iii) risk adjustment release before transition, (iv) acquisition cash flows allocated
− (d) If result is CSM: amount relating to services provided before transition (using coverage units comparison)
Or (e) if result is loss: set loss component to nil and increase liability for remaining coverage

**Acquisition cash flows (C17A):**
Apply C14B–C14D for DPF contracts.

### Insurance finance income or expenses (C18–C19A):

**Groups with contracts issued >1 year apart (C18):**
- May determine locked-in rates at transition date instead of initial recognition (C18(a))
- For OCI disaggregation, cumulative OCI may be determined as: nil (unless DPF with B134 applying, then = cumulative OCI on underlying items) (C18(b))

**Groups without contracts issued >1 year apart (C19):**
- If using C13 for initial recognition rates, also use for B72(b)–(e) rates
- Cumulative OCI determined using C13 rates (for B131), as nil (for B132), using C13 at incurred claim date (for B133), or = cumulative OCI on underlying items (for B134/DPF)

**Interim financial statements (C19A):**
May determine insurance finance amounts as if no interim statements prepared before transition.

## Fair Value Approach

### CSM or loss component (C20):
= Fair value of group at transition date − fulfilment cash flows at transition date
(Do not apply IFRS 13 paragraph 47 for demand features)

### Reinsurance loss-recovery component (C20A–C20B):
= Loss component of underlying contracts at transition × percentage of claims expected to recover
(No need to meet B119C condition — reinsurance need not have been entered before/at time of underlying contract recognition)

### Assessments (C21–C22):
May determine group identification, DPF classification, discretionary cash flows, investment contract DPF classification using:
- (a) Reasonable information about what entity would have determined at inception; OR
- (b) Reasonable information at transition date

May classify settlement claims as liability for incurred claims (C22A).

### Annual cohorts (C23):
Not required to apply paragraph 22 — may include contracts issued >1 year apart.
May determine locked-in rates at transition date instead of initial recognition.

### OCI disaggregation (C24):
Cumulative OCI may be determined:
- (a) Retrospectively — only if reasonable information available; OR
- (b) As nil — unless (c) applies
- (c) For DPF with B134: = cumulative OCI on underlying items

### Asset for insurance acquisition cash flows (C24A–C24B):
Determine at transition at amount equal to acquisition cash flows entity would incur for rights to obtain:
- (a) Recoveries from premiums of contracts issued before transition but not yet recognised
- (b) Future renewals of contracts recognised at transition and contracts in (a)
- (c) Future contracts without re-paying acquisition cash flows already paid

Exclude acquisition cash flow asset amount from measurement of insurance contract groups at transition (C24B).

## Comparative Information

### C25:
May present adjusted comparative information for earlier periods (not required). If so, transition date references shift to beginning of earliest adjusted period.

### C26:
Not required to provide paragraphs 93–132 disclosures for periods before the annual period immediately preceding initial application.

### C27:
Unadjusted comparative information must be clearly identified, with different basis explained.

### C28:
Need not disclose previously unpublished claims development info earlier than 5 years before first applying IFRS 17 (must disclose if not provided).

---

## Classification Overlay (IFRS 17 and IFRS 9 Applied Simultaneously)

### C28A:
Entity first applying IFRS 17 and IFRS 9 at the same time may apply classification overlay for comparative information about financial assets not restated for IFRS 9.

### C28B:
Present comparative information as if IFRS 9 classification/measurement had been applied, using reasonable information at transition date about expected IFRS 9 classification.

### C28C:
Not required to apply IFRS 9 Section 5.5 impairment. If subject to impairment but not applied: continue presenting prior-period IAS 39 impairment amounts.

### C28D:
Differences from classification overlay → opening retained earnings (or other equity) at transition.

### C28E:
Entities applying classification overlay shall:
- (a) Disclose extent of overlay application and impairment treatment
- (b) Apply only to comparative periods between transition date and initial application date
- (c) Apply IFRS 9 transition requirements (Section 7.2) at initial application date

## Redesignation of Financial Assets

### C29 (entity previously applied IFRS 9):
At initial application of IFRS 17, entity:
- (a) **May reassess** SPPI test (4.1.2(a) or 4.1.2A(a)) — only for assets connected to IFRS 17 contracts
- (b) **Shall revoke** FVTPL designation (4.1.5) if condition no longer met due to IFRS 17
- (c) **May designate** FVTPL (4.1.5) if condition now met
- (d) **May designate** equity instrument at FVOCI (5.7.5)
- (e) **May revoke** equity FVOCI designation (5.7.5)

### C30:
Based on facts/circumstances at initial application date. Applied retrospectively using IFRS 9 transition requirements.

### C31:
Not required to restate prior periods (only if possible without hindsight). If not restating: recognise difference between previous and new carrying amount in opening retained earnings.

### C32:
Disclosure requirements: basis for eligible assets, measurement categories and carrying amounts before/after, previously designated FVTPL amounts.

### C33:
Qualitative disclosures about: how C29 applied, reasons for designation/de-designation, reasons for different SPPI conclusions.

### C33A:
For financial assets derecognised between transition date and initial application date, may apply classification overlay (C28B–C28E) as if C29 had been applied.

---

## Withdrawal of IFRS 4

### C34:
IFRS 17 supersedes IFRS 4 Insurance Contracts, as amended in 2020.
